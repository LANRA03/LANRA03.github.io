Turbocharging

    

Turbochargers work by harnessing the wasted energy from exhaust, and using it to pump more air into the engine. Turbos achieve this by placing a turbine in the exhaust manifold. This turbine spins, turning a shaft, which is connected to a compressor wheel, near an ambient air inlet. When the compressor wheel spins, it pulls in air from the inlet, which is then sent to the compressor. The compressor condenses the air, which, as a side effect, heats it up. An intercooler simply cools down the air before it is sent into the engine. The air is then pumped into the fuel mixture, and the cycle repeats.
